Introduction

My name is Nicole Muldoon, and I am a 2nd year student at the University of Aberdeen. As a member of the School of Biological Sciences, I study Zoology full time. I hope to use the knowledge I have learned on this degree to help support Scottish wildlife in the future through conservation work and zookeeping to preserve critically endangered species.

I spent most of my childhood surrounded by animals — dogs, guinea pigs, rabbits, hamsters, fish, frogs and the occassional wild squirrel or deer I'd encounter whilst visiting my grandparents. In my teenage years, I started suffering from illness, and most joy I received was through my pets — they kept me company whilst I couldn't really do much else. That is why I study Zoology — I want to be able to return the favour to animals who perhaps don't have anybody looking out for their health and safety.

I hope through interacting with my website, you are able to learn a little more about me and my studies, and perhaps we can talk in the chat!

In 2018, I began studying HND Animal Care at Dundee and Angus College. This course covered various aspects of animal care, including: parasitology, small animal disease control, first aid, medication and administration of drugs, as well as husbandry, welfare, and wild animal identification. Through this course I also took up a volunteer placement at Blair Drummond Safari Park in Stirling.

I began studying at the University of Aberdeen in 2021, a path that I have thoroughly enjoyed so far. I have already learned important scientific skills, including laboratory work concluding events such as DNA analysis via CRISPR-Cas9 and dissections.
